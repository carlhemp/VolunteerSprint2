Events

Students lives are transformed through events such as retreats and conferences. A volunteer can pull off & even improve the tasks necessary for these vital events.

Event Planner

Help facilitate life-change through coordinating the people and logistics for a retreat, conference, or campus event.

Est Time required: 1-4 hours/week leading up to event

Volunteer Requirements

  • Heart to serve
  • Excellent organizational and communication skills
  • Experience in event planning preferred
  • Agreement with statement of faith and practice may be required
  • Application and/or background check may be required

Volunteers Should Expect to Receive

  • Appropriate training
  • Timely communication
  • Regular coaching and supervision

Greeter

Help students feel welcome as they show up to a conference or campus event.

Est Time required: 1-3 hours, depending on the event

Volunteer Requirements

  • Heart to serve, friendly, punctual

Volunteers Should Expect to Receive

  • Appropriate training
  • Timely communication

A/V Support

Lead or assist in setting up and running the A/V equipment at a conference or campus event

Est Time required: 1-4 hours per event

Volunteer Requirements

  • Heart to serve, willingness to learn
  • Training and experience in running A/V systems preferred

Volunteers Should Expect to Receive

  • Appropriate training
  • Timely communication

A Story of a Church Small Group leading campus events:

Dave and Megan are both on staff full time with Cru. They have been leading in a Metro setting for years but often times without a team. However, they have a great small group that they have been part of for several years. As the members of the small group have heard stories about the Cru ministry in their city they wanted to help.

Every year, Megan and Dave are able to run large outreach events on various campuses only because their small group members help pull off all the details of the event. They have ran large scale evangelistic outreaches similar to the MAZE, E2 forums, and even fall retreats. The small group runs point on all of the logistics of the event about once a year so that Dave and Megan can focus on interaction with current students, new students, and even ministry partners. This has allowed for their small group to really be part of what Dave and Megan are doing and also have given much needed lift to the ministry.

Hospitality

A volunteer can provide the resources or create the environments needed for students to feel completely welcomed into Cru.

A Story of Hospitality:

Gail has recently turned 70 and has more time on her hands as other responsibilities have ended. She is very missional in her neighborhood and has decades of experience volunteering at the Salvation Army, local public schools, and her own community church. She and her Husband are major financial ministry partners but she would also like to volunteer her time in some way.

After meeting with a local Team Leader she has decided that using her home for ministry events, and helping to provide food for outreaches is where she wants to spend her time. Gail has hosted and prepared all the food for a weekend retreat for graduating seniors. A few times a year she provides food for on campus outreaches.

Those outreaches include a Friendsgiving outreaches the dorms, an Evangelistic Christmas Party, and Galentines outreach.. Gail volunteers a few times a year and is faithful to pray for specific events and individual students by name.
